Studying The Park's Eagles

National Park Service biologists study eagles to learn about the health of the river ecosystem. By testing blood and feather samples from chicks they learn about contaminants in the river that may effect all of the animals, including humans, that live in or near the river.

Taking samples from eaglets does not harm them and the parents do not abandon the chicks.
